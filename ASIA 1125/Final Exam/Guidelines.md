**Exam Structure**
## **Part I: Identification Questions — 30%**

You will receive a list of 9–10 id questions (terms, events, historical figures) and choose **three (3)** to answer.

Each answer should be 200–250 words.

For each ID question:

- Describe the term/event/figure
    
- Provide historical context (include key dates)
    
- Explain its significance
    

Write in your own words, NO citations. 

**Evaluation Criteria**

- Accuracy of the fact-based description and understanding of context
    
- Explanation of significance
    
- Clarity and structure
    

_**Rubric**_ (per ID question, 10 points each = 30 points total)**:**

- Outstanding: 10–9
    
- Excellent: 8.9–8
    
- Good: 7.9–7
    
- Satisfactory: 6.9–6
    
- Needs Improvement: 5.9–5
    
- Failed: 4.9–0
    

**Note:** The full list will be available on Brightspace/ Exams, updated to reflect material covered since the midterm exam.

## **Part II: Primary Source Reflection — 30%**

You will be given a short document or video excerpt in the exam.

Write approximately 300-400 words interpreting the source and connecting it to broader historical themes.

Your reflection should:

- Explain the main ideas of the source
    
- Provide historical context
    
- Discuss its significance in Asian history
    
- Follow a coherent structure and clear writing style
    

**Rubric (30 points total):**

- Outstanding: 30–27
    
- Excellent: 26.9–24
    
- Good: 23.9–21
    
- Satisfactory: 20.9–18
    
- Needs Improvement: 17.9–13
    
- Failed: 12.9–0
    

## **Part III: Short Essay — 40%**

Write an essay of 600**–**700 words on a topic chosen from the list, which is to be discussed and uploaded on Brightspace/ Exams.

### **Requirements**

- Develop a clear thesis and argument.
    
- Support your argument with examples and historical reasoning.
    
- NO citations or bibliography required — just articulate your own analysis using class materials and other knowledge.
    
- Your essay must not overlap with your term paper topic.
    

### Evaluation (40 points total)

_**Essay Rubric**_

- Ideas & Argument (8 points)  
    Clarity of thesis, strength of argument, originality.
    
- Content (20 points)  
    Quality of evidence, interpretation of facts, analysis, historical context.
    
- Articulation (Communication): 12 points  
    Organization, clarity, coherence, writing style.
    

# **Academic Integrity**

All work must be your own. Any form of plagiarism or unauthorized assistance will invalidate the exam.

# **Preparation Advice**

- Review lecture notes, slides, assigned readings (Patricia Ebrey, _The Cambridge Illustrated History of China,_ chapters 1-6), class discussions, and assigned primary sources and documentaries.
    
- Focus on understanding broader patterns and historical significance rather than memorizing details.
    

You are strongly encouraged to begin your preparation for the exam.
