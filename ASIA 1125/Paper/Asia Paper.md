[[Syllabus]] 
### **I. Topic**

History of China from antiquity to early modern times, 17-18 cen. 

You are free to choose a topic in any field, including:

- Political history / state formation 
    
- Institutions and governance
    
- Economy and trade
    
- Culture, religion, arts, literature
    
- Social relations / gender / family
    
- International relations and diplomacy
    

Choose a topic on which you can **form an argument**, not just summarize facts.

### **II. Structure and Components**

Your paper must include **three key elements**:

**1. Thesis (argument):**

Your main claim — your interpretation or opinion on a historical issue.

Formulate your argument in one or two sentences in the introduction. 

**2. Narrative (evidence / examples):**

Support your argument with historical evidence, examples, and case studies.

Analyze events/issues in historical context. 

**3. Citations & Bibliography:**

- Use **Chicago-style footnotes** (or another citation style if you prefer — just be consistent).
    
- Include a **bibliography** at the end listing _all_ sources used.
    
- Use **at least 5 academic sources** (books, scholarly articles, or archival/primary documents).  
    _Encyclopedia entries do not count._
    
- You may use **both primary and secondary sources** (primary sources are not required).
    
- You may use **non-English sources** — just cite them properly and include an **English translation of the title**.
    
- **Online sources** are allowed, as long as they are credible. 
    
- Do **NOT** cite class materials (the textbook, lecture notes, or slides).
    

**AI Policy:**  
You may **consult** AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT). You may include **no more than two citations** of AI-generated content in your paper.  
Any AI-generated content must be:

- properly cited in footnotes, and
    
- listed in the bibliography.
    

_Example (Chicago style footnote):_

> ChatGPT, response to “What happened during the Sui-Koguryo Wars,” OpenAI, accessed Nov. 3, 2025.

**Important:** Any false, inaccurate, or fabricated information (“hallucinations”) produced by an AI tool is **your responsibility**. You must verify all facts and sources used in your paper.

**Format:**

- 5–6 pages, double-spaced
    
- 12 pt font (Times New Roman or similar)
    
- Word file (.docx)
    
- Subheadings encouraged (e.g., Introduction, Conclusion)
    
- You may include images, maps, charts — just cite the source
    

### **III. Academic Integrity**

- Your paper will be checked by **Turnitin**.
    
- All ideas and quotations must be **properly cited**.
    
- Plagiarism (similarity) or/and AI-generated writing submitted as your own is not allowed and will disqualify the paper.
    
- **Submit your own writing and your own thinking**.
